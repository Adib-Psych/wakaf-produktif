Dua karung ceri merah penuh dari Kebun 2, dipetik di hari yang sama, di-pulp di pulper yang sama, dengan air yang sama. Tetapi setelah dua minggu drying: karung A menghasilkan green bean bersih, biru-hijau seragam, profil cup floral-citrus. Karung B menghasilkan green bean dengan bercak putih (silver skin lengket), profil cup fermenty-vinegar dengan defect 8%. Apa yang berbeda? Bukan ceri-nya, bukan pulpernya. Yang berbeda adalah fase fermentasi dan drying-nya — durasi, suhu, dan handling-nya.

Two sacks of fully-red cherries from Block 2, picked the same day, pulped at the same pulper, with the same water. But after two weeks of drying: sack A produces clean green beans, uniform blue-green, with a floral-citrus cup profile. Sack B produces green beans with white patches (sticky silver skin), a fermenty-vinegar cup profile, and 8% defect. What is different? Not the cherries, not the pulper. The difference lies in the fermentation and drying phases — duration, temperature, and handling.

Setelah Modul O mengunci buah mana yang dipetik dan Modul P mengunci kapan harus diproses, modul ini menjawab pertanyaan terakhir di pasca-panen primer: bagaimana mengubah ceri jadi green bean. Jawabannya adalah empat fase berurutan: Pulping (kupas kulit) → Fermentation (urai mucilage 12-36 jam) → Washing (bersih) → Drying (turunkan kadar air ke 12-12,5%). Tidak ada fase yang bisa dilompati. Tidak ada short-cut yang menjaga mutu. Setiap fase punya endpoint yang dapat diukur, dan endpoint itulah yang menentukan apakah fase berikutnya bisa dimulai.

After Module O locked in which fruit to pick and Module P locked in when to process, this module answers the final question of primary post-harvest: how to turn cherries into green beans. The answer is four sequential phases: Pulping (skin removal) → Fermentation (12-36h mucilage breakdown) → Washing (clean) → Drying (lower moisture to 12-12.5%). No phase can be skipped. No shortcut preserves quality. Each phase has a measurable endpoint, and that endpoint determines whether the next phase can begin.

Modul ini ditulis oleh seorang psikolog klinis yang sedang belajar agronomi kopi dari awal. Pendekatannya: meminjam konsep phase-based treatment protocol dari psikoterapi trauma-focused. Terapi PTSD klasik (Herman 1992 + Cloitre et al 2002) terdiri dari tiga fase yang ketat berurutan: (1) Stabilization — bangun keamanan dan regulasi emosi. (2) Processing — kerja terapeutik pada memori traumatis. (3) Integration — reklamasi koneksi sosial dan makna. Tidak ada fase yang bisa dilompati — pasien yang langsung masuk processing tanpa stabilization sering decompensate. Pasca-panen kopi mengikuti logika yang identik.

This module is written by a clinical psychologist learning coffee agronomy from scratch. The approach: borrow phase-based treatment protocol from trauma-focused psychotherapy. Classical PTSD treatment (Herman 1992 + Cloitre et al 2002) consists of three strictly sequential phases: (1) Stabilization — build safety and emotion regulation. (2) Processing — therapeutic work on traumatic memories. (3) Integration — reclaiming social connection and meaning. No phase can be skipped — patients who jump straight to processing without stabilization often decompensate. Coffee post-harvest follows identical logic.

Konteks Kebun Wakaf NgantangThe Ngantang Waqf Garden Context

Kebun kopi wakaf Ngantang berada di elevasi 648 mdpl dengan iklim tropis lembab — curah hujan tahunan 1.800-2.500 mm dan kelembapan udara 75-85% sepanjang tahun. Untuk pasca-panen, dua faktor lokasi ini punya implikasi spesifik: (1) Drying lebih lambat dari kebun dataran rendah — udara lembab artinya gradien tekanan uap kecil, jadi waktu drying 12-16 hari (vs 7-10 hari di Sumatra/Lampung). (2) Risiko jamur tinggi kalau drying tidak tuntas — Aspergillus ochraceus dan Penicillium tumbuh subur di kondisi MC residu 13-15% dengan kelembapan udara >80%, menghasilkan toksin ochratoxin A (regulasi EU max 5 ppb di green bean).

The Ngantang waqf coffee garden sits at 648 m elevation with a humid tropical climate — 1,800-2,500 mm annual rainfall and 75-85% air humidity year-round. For post-harvest, two location factors have specific implications: (1) Drying is slower than in lowland gardens — humid air means a small vapor pressure gradient, so drying takes 12-16 days (vs 7-10 days in Sumatra/Lampung). (2) High mould risk if drying is incomplete — Aspergillus ochraceus and Penicillium thrive at residual MC 13-15% with >80% air humidity, producing ochratoxin A (EU regulatory max 5 ppb in green bean).

In Ngantang, the primary post-harvest strategy for Cycle 2026-2027 is a hybrid offtaker approach: specialty-grade cherries (≥70% fully red, quick-sorted at the garden) go to Bonsari Coffee for full processing (pulping → fermentation → washing → drying) at their pulper. Implikasinya: pasca-panen bukan langkah "ditambahkan" setelah panen, melainkan 50% dari nilai biji. Petik ceri merah penuh terbaik dunia (Modul O) yang dirusak fermentasi over-shoot atau drying tidak tuntas hanya menghasilkan commodity-grade. Sebaliknya, ceri rata-rata yang diproses dengan 4 fase disiplin endpoint dapat naik ke specialty-grade ringan. Modul ini sangat penting untuk dikuasai oleh Wakif & tim sebelum musim panen 2026 — supaya keputusan handoff ke Bonsari, atau processing parsial di rumah Wakif, dapat dibuat berdasarkan pemahaman teknis solid, bukan hanya intuisi.

The implication: post-harvest is not a step "added" after harvest, but 50% of the bean's value. The world's best fully-red cherries (Module O) ruined by fermentation over-shoot or incomplete drying yield only commodity grade. Conversely, average cherries processed with disciplined four-phase endpoints can rise to light-specialty grade. This Module deserves mastery by the Waqif and team before the 2026 harvest season — so handoff decisions to Bonsari, or partial in-house processing at the Waqif's home, can be made on solid technical understanding, not just intuition.

Teori Dasar: Empat Fase Pasca-Panen PrimerTheory: The Four Phases of Primary Post-Harvest

schema building

Mengapa empat fase, bukan satu langkah tunggal "olah biji"? Karena setiap fase punya tujuan biokimia berbeda dan endpoint yang spesifik. Mencampurnya jadi satu langkah (misalnya "rendam ceri sehari, jemur") menghasilkan profil cup yang tidak terkontrol dan sering cacat. Empat fase yang dipisahkan dengan jelas membuat setiap variabel dapat dikontrol — dan kalau ada cacat, dapat diidentifikasi fase mana yang menjadi sumber masalah.

Empat fase pasca-panen primer untuk metode fully washed (yang dipilih kebun kopi wakaf Ngantang sebagai default specialty):

  • Fase 1 · Pulping — kupas kulit ceri (exocarp) dengan pulper mekanik. Tujuan: pisahkan biji+mucilage (lapisan lendir manis) dari kulit. Endpoint: kulit hilang, biji utuh dengan mucilage masih melekat, broken bean <3%. Durasi: instant. Dilakukan di pulper mitra (Bonsari Coffee) untuk batch specialty.
  • Fase 2 · Fermentation — biji ber-mucilage di-tampung dalam tank/karung berlubang 12-36 jam (suhu lokal Ngantang 24-28°C). Tujuan: enzim pektinase alami + mikroflora memecah pektin di mucilage. Endpoint: slip test positive — saat digenggam, biji terasa "kesat" tidak lagi licin. Durasi rule-of-thumb: 18-24 jam untuk Robusta NGT (suhu sedang). Over-shoot (>48 jam) → profil acetic/vinegar.
  • Fase 3 · Washing — biji slip-test-positive dicuci dengan air bersih untuk menghilangkan sisa mucilage. Tujuan: hentikan fermentasi + bersihkan permukaan biji. Endpoint: air bilasan jernih, biji tidak lagi licin saat digenggam. Durasi: 5-15 menit + 2-3 kali ganti air.
  • Fase 4 · Drying — biji ber-parchment dijemur di raised bed (atau lantai semen) di bawah sinar matahari dengan reversal teratur. Tujuan: turunkan kadar air (MC) dari ~55% (post-wash) ke 12-12,5% (target shelf-stable). Endpoint: MC reading di moisture meter = 12-12,5% (toleransi ±0,5%) dan water activity <0,65. Durasi NGT (humid climate): 12-16 hari sun-dry, atau 36-48 jam mechanical dryer.

Why four phases, not a single "process the bean" step? Because each phase has a distinct biochemical purpose and a specific endpoint. Collapsing them into one (e.g. "soak cherries a day, then dry") produces uncontrolled cup profiles and frequent defects. Four cleanly separated phases make each variable controllable — and if a defect arises, it can be traced to the phase that caused it.

Four phases of primary post-harvest using the fully washed method (chosen as default specialty at the Ngantang waqf coffee garden):

  • Phase 1 · Pulping — strip the cherry skin (exocarp) with a mechanical pulper. Purpose: separate bean+mucilage (sweet sticky layer) from skin. Endpoint: skin removed, bean intact with mucilage still attached, broken beans <3%. Duration: instant. Done at the partner pulper (Bonsari Coffee) for specialty batches.
  • Phase 2 · Fermentation — mucilage-coated beans held in tanks/ventilated sacks for 12-36 hours (Ngantang ambient temp 24-28°C). Purpose: natural pectinase + microflora break down pectin in the mucilage. Endpoint: slip test positive — when grasped, the bean feels "raspy" no longer slick. Rule-of-thumb: 18-24 hours for NGT Robusta (mild temp). Over-shoot (>48h) → acetic/vinegar profile.
  • Phase 3 · Washing — slip-positive beans are washed with clean water to remove mucilage residue. Purpose: halt fermentation + clean bean surface. Endpoint: rinse water clear, beans no longer slick when grasped. Duration: 5-15 minutes + 2-3 water changes.
  • Phase 4 · Drying — parchment beans dried on raised beds (or concrete floor) under sun with regular turning. Purpose: lower moisture content (MC) from ~55% (post-wash) to 12-12.5% (shelf-stable target). Endpoint: moisture meter reading = 12-12.5% (tolerance ±0.5%) and water activity <0.65. NGT duration (humid climate): 12-16 days sun-dry, or 36-48 hours mechanical dryer.

Kenapa endpoint per fase begitu penting? Karena pasca-panen tidak bisa di-"timeline by clock" semata — tergantung temperatur ambient, populasi mikroba awal, dan ketebalan mucilage. Yang konsisten adalah kondisi akhir biji, bukan jam-nya. Fermentasi yang "selesai" di Sumatra 14 jam mungkin baru "selesai" di Ngantang 22 jam karena suhu lokal lebih rendah. Drying yang "selesai" di musim kering Lampung 8 hari bisa butuh 16 hari di Ngantang musim hujan. Yang membuat profil cup konsisten antar batch adalah endpoint criterion: slip test positive, MC 12-12,5%, water activity <0,65 — bukan durasi jam. Tim lapangan dilatih membaca endpoint, bukan menghitung jam.

Why are per-phase endpoints so important? Because post-harvest cannot be "timed by the clock" alone — it depends on ambient temperature, initial microbe load, and mucilage thickness. What stays consistent is the end-state of the bean, not the hours. Fermentation that "completes" in 14 hours in Sumatra may only complete at 22 hours in Ngantang due to lower local temperature. Drying that "finishes" in 8 days during Lampung's dry season can take 16 days in Ngantang's rainy season. What makes the cup profile consistent across batches is the endpoint criterion: slip test positive, MC 12-12.5%, water activity <0.65 — not hour count. Field teams are trained to read the endpoint, not count hours.

Analogi dari Psikologi Klinis — Phase-Based Treatment Protocol (Herman 1992, Cloitre et al 2002)An Analogy from Clinical Psychology — Phase-Based Treatment Protocol (Herman 1992, Cloitre et al 2002)

Dalam terapi trauma kompleks, ada konsensus klinis sejak Judith Herman (Trauma & Recovery, 1992) bahwa pemulihan trauma tidak bisa langsung ke pemrosesan memori tanpa terlebih dahulu membangun keamanan. Tiga fase wajib berurutan: (1) Stabilization — keamanan, regulasi emosi, koneksi dasar (rule-of-thumb durasi: bulan ke-bulan, bukan minggu). (2) Processing — kerja terapeutik pada memori traumatis (sering pakai EMDR / TF-CBT / Prolonged Exposure). (3) Integration — reklamasi koneksi sosial, makna, dan identitas pasca-trauma. Endpoint per fase: pasien yang masih disregulasi emosi tidak boleh masuk fase 2 — risikonya re-traumatisasi.

In complex trauma therapy, clinical consensus since Judith Herman (Trauma & Recovery, 1992) holds that trauma recovery cannot jump straight to memory processing without first establishing safety. Three mandatory sequential phases: (1) Stabilization — safety, emotion regulation, basic connection (rule-of-thumb duration: months not weeks). (2) Processing — therapeutic work on traumatic memories (often EMDR / TF-CBT / Prolonged Exposure). (3) Integration — reclaiming social connection, meaning, post-trauma identity. Per-phase endpoint: a patient still emotion-dysregulated should not enter phase 2 — the risk is re-traumatization.

Pasca-panen kopi mengikuti logika yang persis identik. Empat fase berurutan dengan endpoint per fase — tidak ada fase yang bisa dilompati: (1) Pulping setara stabilization — pemisahan dasar biji dari kulit (foundation). (2) Fermentation setara processing — kerja biokimia di mucilage yang membentuk profil cup (memerlukan kontrol dan tidak boleh over-shoot, sama seperti processing trauma yang harus dosed). (3) Washing setara fase transisi processing→integration — menghentikan kerja aktif, membersihkan. (4) Drying setara integration — biji yang shelf-stable, siap masuk rantai distribusi (analog pasien yang siap kembali ke masyarakat dengan integrasi makna). Setiap fase punya endpoint sendiri — petik yang baik (Modul O) saja tidak cukup; window timing (Modul P) saja tidak cukup; harus empat fase ini lewat dengan benar.

Coffee post-harvest follows precisely identical logic. Four sequential phases with per-phase endpoints — no phase can be skipped: (1) Pulping parallels stabilization — basic separation of bean from skin (foundation). (2) Fermentation parallels processing — biochemical work in the mucilage that shapes cup profile (requires control and must not over-shoot, like trauma processing that must be dosed). (3) Washing parallels the processing→integration transition — halting active work, cleaning. (4) Drying parallels integration — shelf-stable beans ready to enter distribution (analog of a patient ready to re-enter society with integrated meaning). Each phase has its own endpoint — good picking (Module O) alone is not enough; timing window (Module P) alone is not enough; all four phases must pass correctly.

✓ Tiga Kesalahan Umum yang Menggagalkan Pasca-Panen✓ Three Common Mistakes that Derail Post-Harvest

(1) Fermentation over-shoot (>48 jam) — Biji ditinggal di tank sampai keesokan harinya "supaya aman tuntas". Yang terjadi: bakteri acetic-acid mengubah mucilage jadi cuka, profil cup bergeser ke vinegar/fermenty. Solusi: cek slip test setiap 4-6 jam mulai jam-12, segera washing saat positive. (2) Drying terlalu cepat / kepanasan — Dijemur di lantai semen panas tanpa naungan, MC turun terlalu cepat dari luar. Kulit luar biji kering sementara dalamnya masih basah → cracking + retak yang akan jadi defect green bean. Solusi: raised bed dengan sirkulasi udara, suhu tidak >40°C, reversal setiap 1-2 jam. (3) Drying tidak tuntas (MC residu 13-15%) — Karena warna sudah "terlihat kering" lalu dikemas. Yang terjadi: jamur Aspergillus + Penicillium tumbuh dalam 2-4 minggu di karung tertutup, menghasilkan ochratoxin A (regulasi EU max 5 ppb). Solusi: WAJIB pakai moisture meter, target 12-12,5% (toleransi ±0,5%).

(1) Fermentation over-shoot (>48h) — Beans left in tank overnight "to make sure it's done". What happens: acetic-acid bacteria convert mucilage to vinegar, cup profile shifts to vinegar/fermenty. Solution: slip test every 4-6 hours from hour-12 onwards, wash immediately when positive. (2) Drying too fast / overheated — Spread on hot concrete floor with no shade, MC drops too fast from the outside. Outer skin dries while interior still wet → cracking + cracked green bean defects. Solution: raised beds with air circulation, temp not >40°C, turn every 1-2 hours. (3) Incomplete drying (residual MC 13-15%) — Packaged because the color "looks dry". What happens: Aspergillus + Penicillium mould grows within 2-4 weeks in closed sacks, producing ochratoxin A (EU regulatory max 5 ppb). Solution: MANDATORY moisture meter, target 12-12.5% (tolerance ±0.5%).

Mengapa Giling Kering, Bukan Giling Basah?Why Dry Hulling, Not Wet Hulling?

trade-off analysis

Di Fase 4 (Drying) di atas, kami menyebutkan target MC 12-12,5% sebelum dianggap "selesai". Ada pertanyaan logis: kenapa harus tunggu MC sangat rendah baru di-hull? Bukankah lebih cepat di-hull saat MC masih 30-50%? Jawabannya menyentuh satu titik divergensi utama di dunia pasca-panen kopi: giling kering (dry hulling) versus giling basah (wet hulling / Giling Basah Indonesia).

In Phase 4 (Drying) above, we mentioned the MC 12-12.5% target before declaring "complete". A logical question: why wait for very low MC before hulling? Wouldn't it be faster to hull at MC 30-50%? The answer touches on one of the major divergences in coffee post-harvest: dry hulling versus wet hulling (Giling Basah Indonesia).

Giling Basah (sering disingkat GB) adalah praktek regional Indonesia, paling terkenal dari Sumatra (Aceh Gayo, Mandailing) untuk Arabika. Karakteristik: parchment di-hull saat MC masih 30-50% (belum kering tuntas), kemudian green bean dijemur lagi sampai MC 12,5%. Metode ini menghasilkan profil cup yang sangat distinctive: body kuat, acidity rendah, earthy/woody/herbal notes — sering disebut "Sumatra style" oleh roaster dunia. Green bean-nya berwarna biru-hijau intens yang khas, berbeda dari hijau pucat standar specialty global.

Wet hulling (Indonesian Giling Basah / GB) is a regional Indonesian practice, most famously from Sumatra (Aceh Gayo, Mandailing) for Arabica. Characteristics: parchment is hulled while MC is still 30-50% (not yet fully dry), then the green bean is dried further to MC 12.5%. This method produces a distinctly different cup profile: heavy body, low acidity, earthy/woody/herbal notes — often called "Sumatra style" by global roasters. Its green bean has the signature intense blue-green color, different from the pale-green standard of global specialty.

Apa Kata SOP ResmiWhat the Official SOP Says

SOP Kementan/SCOPI Robusta Edisi 2 (2019), hal 234-236, hanya menyebutkan satu metode untuk proses basah (fully washed): pulping → fermentation → washing → pengeringan ke MC 12,5% → hulling (dilakukan saat biji sudah kering) → sortasi biji kering → pengemasan. Quote eksplisit: "Biji kopi HS yang baru selesai dikeringkan harus terlebih dahulu didinginkan sampai suhu ruangan sebelum dikupas." Puslitbang 2010 (hal 61) konvergen: "Biji kopi kering atau kopi HS kering digiling dengan mesin huller untuk mendapatkan biji kopi pasar."

The Indonesian Ministry of Agriculture / SCOPI Robusta SOP Edition 2 (2019), pages 234-236, mentions only one method for the wet process (fully washed): pulping → fermentation → washing → drying to MC 12.5% → hulling (performed when beans are already dry) → dry bean sorting → packaging. The explicit quote: "Coffee HS beans that have just finished drying must first be cooled to room temperature before being hulled." Puslitbang 2010 (page 61) converges: "Dry coffee beans or dry HS coffee are hulled with a huller machine to obtain market coffee beans."

Tidak ada SOP yang merekomendasikan giling basah untuk Robusta specialty. Giling Basah berkembang sebagai praktek lokal Sumatra di luar kerangka SOP nasional, dengan latar belakang historis: musim panen Sumatra sering bersamaan dengan musim hujan panjang, sehingga drying tuntas susah dicapai; petani memilih giling parsial supaya parchment bisa disimpan lebih cepat sambil menunggu cuaca cerah. Ini adalah adaptasi pragmatis, bukan upgrade kualitas.

No SOP recommends wet hulling for Robusta specialty. Wet hulling developed as a local Sumatra practice outside the national SOP framework, with historical context: Sumatra's harvest season often coincides with extended rainy seasons, making complete drying difficult; farmers chose partial hulling so parchment could be stored faster while waiting for clear weather. This is a pragmatic adaptation, not a quality upgrade.

Tiga Alasan Klinis SOP Tidak Adopt Giling BasahThree Clinical Reasons SOP Does Not Adopt Wet Hulling

  1. Risiko jamur ochratoxin lebih tinggi. Hulling pada MC 30-50% membuka green bean ke udara di kondisi yang masih lembab. Aspergillus ochraceus dan Penicillium tumbuh subur di rentang MC 13-18% dengan kelembapan udara >80%. Kalau drying lanjutan tidak konsisten (sering terjadi di iklim humid), populasi jamur sudah internal di biji sebelum MC turun ke level aman. Hasil: ochratoxin A (mycotoxin yang regulated EU max 5 ppb di green bean). Batch reject untuk pasar Eropa.
  2. Defect rate naik. Biji setengah-kering (MC 30-50%) lebih lunak dan mudah pecah saat melewati huller. Broken bean rate naik dari ~3% (giling kering proper) ke 10-15% (giling basah). Broken bean adalah cacat utama di grading SNI Mutu 1 dan 2 — batch dengan broken bean tinggi otomatis turun ke Mutu 3 atau commodity.
  3. Profil cup tidak match strategi offtaker. Giling Basah menghasilkan body kuat + earthy yang dicari oleh segmen tertentu (Italian espresso blender, traditional roaster Sumatra-style). Tetapi mitra kami — Bonsari Coffee dan Nomad Coffee — adalah specialty roaster yang mencari profil clean, bright, terroir-driven. Mengirim batch giling basah ke mereka = mismatch ekspektasi, kemungkinan reject atau downgrade harga.
  1. Higher ochratoxin mould risk. Hulling at MC 30-50% exposes the green bean to air in still-humid conditions. Aspergillus ochraceus and Penicillium thrive in the MC 13-18% range with >80% air humidity. If continued drying is inconsistent (common in humid climates), mould populations are already internal to the bean before MC drops to safe levels. Result: ochratoxin A (a mycotoxin regulated to a 5 ppb max in green bean by the EU). Batches rejected for European markets.
  2. Higher defect rate. Semi-dry beans (MC 30-50%) are softer and break more easily as they pass through the huller. Broken bean rate rises from ~3% (proper dry hulling) to 10-15% (wet hulling). Broken beans are the primary defect in SNI Grade 1 and 2 grading — batches with high broken bean counts automatically drop to Grade 3 or commodity.
  3. Cup profile mismatches the offtaker strategy. Wet hulling produces the heavy-body + earthy profile sought by certain segments (Italian espresso blenders, traditional Sumatra-style roasters). But our partners — Bonsari Coffee and Nomad Coffee — are specialty roasters seeking clean, bright, terroir-driven profiles. Sending a wet-hulled batch to them = expectation mismatch, likely rejection or price downgrade.

Pelajaran Lapangan: Strategi Multi-Batch Drying dengan Lahan TerbatasField Lesson: Multi-Batch Drying Strategy with Limited Space

case study · operational excellence

Section ini dibangun dari pelajaran lapangan kebun wakaf Ngantang (04 Jun 2026) — dengan harapan petani lain yang menghadapi kendala sama bisa belajar dari konteks operasional yang nyata. Pertanyaan kunci yang sering muncul: "Lahan jemur saya terbatas, panen datang bertahap, bolehkah saya campur beberapa batch di area drying yang sama?"

This section is built from field lessons at the Ngantang waqf garden (04 Jun 2026) — with the hope that other farmers facing similar constraints can learn from real operational context. The key question that often arises: "My drying space is limited, harvest comes in stages, may I mix several batches in the same drying area?"

Dua Skenario yang Sering Tercampur — Konsekuensinya Sangat BerbedaTwo Scenarios That Are Often Confused — With Very Different Consequences

🔴 Skenario A — Stop-and-Go (Hindari)🔴 Scenario A — Stop-and-Go (Avoid)

Apa yang terjadi: Batch 1 dijemur sampai semi-kering (MC 15-17%) → DISIMPAN dalam karung berhari-hari → fokus jemur Batch 2-3 dalam keadaan basah → setelah semua siap, dikumpulkan dan dijemur ulang bareng.

Mengapa berbahaya: MC 15-17% adalah zona bahaya jamur (Aspergillus ochraceus) → ochratoxin A dalam 3-7 hari. Plus stress-cracking biji + fermenty off-flavor + re-wetting moisture migration permanen. Verdict: jangan.

What happens: Batch 1 is dried until semi-dry (MC 15-17%) → STORED in sacks for days → focus drying Batch 2-3 while wet → after all are ready, all combined and re-dried together.

Why dangerous: MC 15-17% is the mould danger zone (Aspergillus ochraceus) → ochratoxin A within 3-7 days. Plus stress-cracking + fermenty off-flavor + permanent re-wetting moisture migration. Verdict: do not.

🟢 Skenario B — Concurrent 1-Hari-Gap (OK)🟢 Scenario B — Concurrent 1-Day-Gap (OK)

Apa yang terjadi: Batch 1 (hari ke-5) + Batch 2 (hari ke-4) + Batch 3 (hari ke-3) dijemur concurrent di area yang sama. Gap konsisten 1 hari antar batch. Tidak ada fresh cherry yang dimasukkan ke batch lama.

Mengapa OK: MC differential maksimum ~12% antara batch tertua dan termuda → masih dalam window aman. Continuous drying = tidak ada zona bahaya jamur. Cocok dengan rekomendasi Wintgens 2004 Ch. 24: "Lots dried within a 48-hour MC window can be safely co-located." Verdict: operational excellence.

What happens: Batch 1 (day 5) + Batch 2 (day 4) + Batch 3 (day 3) are dried concurrently in the same area. Consistent 1-day gap between batches. No fresh cherries are added to existing batches.

Why OK: Maximum MC differential ~12% between oldest and newest batch → within safe window. Continuous drying = no mould danger zone. Aligns with Wintgens 2004 Ch. 24 recommendation: "Lots dried within a 48-hour MC window can be safely co-located." Verdict: operational excellence.

Tiga Disiplin Operasional yang Wajib Dijaga (Skenario B)Three Operational Disciplines to Maintain (Scenario B)

Mixing concurrent 1-hari-gap aman HANYA JIKA tiga disiplin di bawah ini dipertahankan. Tanpa disiplin ini, konsekuensi skenario Stop-and-Go bisa muncul kembali bahkan pada setup yang awalnya benar.

Concurrent 1-day-gap mixing is safe ONLY IF the three disciplines below are maintained. Without them, Stop-and-Go consequences can re-emerge even from an initially correct setup.

  1. Endpoint Sequential Pull-Out — Batch tertua ditarik dari jemuran saat capai MC 12,5%, walaupun batch lain belum siap. Jangan tahan Batch 1 sampai semua batch siap berbarengan; konsekuensi: Batch 1 over-dried, biji brittle, broken bean naik saat hulling, bobot loss = revenue loss.
  2. Maintain Identitas Lot — Setiap batch punya marker (chalk number, tag tarp, atau warna terpal beda). Tetap dipisah saat dikumpulkan ke karung. Jangan mix fisik kecuali memang intentional blend lot; konsekuensi: cup quality jadi rata-rata, kehilangan upside dari batch terbaik.
  3. Moisture Monitor Per Batch Zone — Sampel ambil dari setiap batch zone secara terpisah, bukan satu sampel campuran. Catat di log harian (tanggal, batch ID, MC reading, jam pengukuran); konsekuensi: salah baca endpoint, salah satu batch under-dried (risk mould) atau over-dried (revenue loss).
  1. Endpoint Sequential Pull-Out — Pull the oldest batch off the drying yard when it reaches 12.5% MC, even if other batches are not ready. Do not hold Batch 1 until all batches are synchronously ready; consequence: Batch 1 becomes over-dried, beans brittle, broken-bean rate rises at hulling, weight loss = revenue loss.
  2. Maintain Lot Identity — Each batch has a marker (chalk number, tarp tag, or different colored tarp). Keep them separate when collected into sacks. Do not mix physically unless an intentional blend lot is desired; consequence: cup quality becomes averaged, losing the upside of the best batch.
  3. Per-Batch Zone Moisture Monitoring — Take samples from each batch zone separately, not one mixed sample. Log daily (date, batch ID, MC reading, time of measurement); consequence: misreading endpoint, one batch under-dried (mould risk) or over-dried (revenue loss).

Sortasi Multi-Dimensi yang Sudah Sesuai SOPMulti-Dimensional Sorting Already Aligned with SOP

Praktek lapangan kebun wakaf Ngantang menunjukkan sortasi sebelum drying sudah dilakukan pada tiga dimensi: (1) warna (Petik Merah, Overripped, Orange under-ripe, Hijau under-ripe), (2) density (biji mengambang dari sortasi air = floaters), dan (3) maturasi (waktu petik berbeda → batch berbeda dengan gap konsisten). Ini sesuai dengan SOP Kementan/SCOPI Robusta 2019 hal 273 (sortasi visual + density-based) — bahkan lebih advanced dari standar minimum SCOPI karena split lot fisik dipertahankan sepanjang drying process.

Field practice at the Ngantang waqf garden shows sorting before drying is already done on three dimensions: (1) color (Red picked, Overripped, Orange under-ripe, Green under-ripe), (2) density (floating beans from water sorting = floaters), and (3) maturation (different picking times → different batches with consistent gap). This aligns with the Kementan/SCOPI Robusta SOP 2019 p273 (visual + density-based sorting) — and is even more advanced than minimum SCOPI standards because physical lot separation is maintained throughout the drying process.

🌾 Catatan untuk Petani Lain dengan Lahan Jemur Terbatas

Jika lahan jemur terbatas tetapi panen datang bertahap, boleh share drying area dengan multiple batches selama: (a) gap waktu antar penambahan batch konsisten dan ≤ 48 jam, (b) tidak ada fresh cherry yang dimasukkan ke batch yang sudah berjalan lama, (c) tiga disiplin operasional di atas dijaga, (d) lot identity dipertahankan dengan marker visual. Yang HARUS dihindari mutlak adalah Stop-and-Go (simpan biji semi-kering berhari-hari kemudian jemur ulang) — ini zona bahaya jamur dan tidak ada koreksi.

If drying space is limited but harvest comes in stages, you may share drying area with multiple batches as long as: (a) the time gap between batch additions is consistent and ≤ 48 hours, (b) no fresh cherries are added to a batch that has been running for a long time, (c) the three operational disciplines above are maintained, (d) lot identity is preserved with visual markers. What MUST be absolutely avoided is Stop-and-Go (storing semi-dry beans for days then re-drying) — this is the mould danger zone with no correction possible.

Endpoint = MC 12-12.5% (tolerance ±0.5%), measured with a calibrated green bean moisture meter (Sinar AP-6060 or equivalent). Ngantang humid duration: 12-16 days sun-dry. DO NOT rely on visual "looks dry" — ochratoxin mould risk. Waqif audit: minimum 2 visits — day 5 (MC ~30%) and day 12 (MC ~14%, near endpoint). Bring own moisture meter to cross-check Bonsari if needed.
